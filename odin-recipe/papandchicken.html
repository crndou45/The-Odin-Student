Pap and Chicken Description

A classic South African pap and chicken meal consists of a rich, flavorful chicken stew (sishebo) served alongside a firm or soft maize meal porridge (pap).

Ingredients

  1. 1 kg chicken pieces (drumsticks and thighs work well)

  2. 30 ml (2 tbsp) sunflower oil

  3. 1 large onion, finely chopped

  4. 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped

  5. 1 tbsp ginger paste (optional)

  6. 1 tbsp Rajah mild & spicy curry powder (or a similar curry powder)

  7. 1 tsp chicken spice mix (e.g., Robertsons chicken spice)

  8. 2 x 400g tins chopped and peeled tomatoes

  9. 500 ml (2 cups) water or chicken stock

  10. Salt and pepper to taste

  11. 1 packet brown onion soup powder (for thickening)

  12. Fresh parsley or coriander, chopped, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare Chicken

    • Season and brown the chicken: Season the chicken pieces with salt, pepper, and the chicken spice mix. Heat the oil in a large pot or casserole dish over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces and brown them on all sides. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.

    • Saut√© aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion and fry until soft. Add the garlic and ginger paste (if using) and the curry powder, frying for another minute until fragrant.

    • Cook the chicken and potatoes: Return the browned chicken to the pot. Add the potato chunks. Cover the pot and let it simmer gently for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the potatoes are soft.

    • Thicken the gravy: If a thicker gravy is desired, mix the brown onion soup powder with a little cold water to form a smooth paste. Stir this into the stew and let it simmer for a further 5-10 minutes until the gravy has thickened.

    • Serve: Adjust seasoning if needed. Garnish with fresh parsley or coriander and serve hot with the pap.

Ingredients

  1. 4 cups water

  2. 2 cups maize meal

  3. 1 tsp salt

  4. 1 tbsp butter (optional, for creaminess)

Instructions

  1. Prepare Pap

    • Boil water: In a heavy-based pot, bring the water to a rolling boil and add the salt and butter (if using).

    • Add maize meal: Gradually pour the maize meal into the boiling water, allowing it to form a mound in the center. Do not stir immediately.

    • Simmer and stir: Cover the pot with a lid and reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer for about 10 minutes.

    • Stir well: Remove the lid and stir vigorously with a wooden spoon or a fork to break up any lumps and incorporate the maize meal evenly. The mixture will be stiff.

    • Steam: Cover the pot again and continue to cook on low heat for another 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally (every few minutes) to ensure even cooking and prevent sticking

    • Fluff and serve: Once cooked and firm, fluff the pap with a fork before serving it alongside your chicken stew.
