Kota Description

A kota is a popular South African street food made by hollowing out a quarter-loaf of bread and filling it with various ingredients, most commonly chips (fries), atchar (spicy mango pickle), sausage, and a fried egg

Ingredients

  1. /4 loaf white bread: The edible container for your fillings.

  2. 100g fried chips (fries): A core component.

  3. 1 Russian or Vienna sausage: Fried.

  4. 1-2 slices cheese: Cheddar or mozzarella.

  5. 1 extra large egg: Fried to your preference (runny is popular).

  6. 45-60 ml Atchar: Mango chutney for a tangy kick.

  7. Optional add-ons: Polony, ham, lettuce, tomato slices, chakalaka, or a beef patty.

  8. Sauces: Mayonnaise, ketchup, mustard, and/or a "Kasi flavour sauce"

Instructions

  1. Prepare the fillings:
    • Chips: Peel and cut potatoes into chips, then deep-fry until golden brown and crispy. Drain and season with salt and chip spice.

    • Sausage: Fry the Russian or Vienna sausage in a pan until cooked and flavorful.

    • Egg: Fry the egg in the same pan, to preference

    • (Optional) Prepare any other fillings, such as frying a beef patty or caramelizing onions.

  2. Prepare the bread:

    • Cut a quarter from a loaf of white bread. Remove the soft inner part, leaving a thick crust container.

    • Toast the hollowed-out bread (both the crust and the removed inner part) in a pan or oven until golden brown.

  3. Assemble the kota::

    • Spread your choice of sauce (mayonnaise, ketchup, etc.) on the inside of the hollowed-out bread.

    • Layer the ingredients inside the bread: Start with the fried chips, then add the sausage and cheese slices.

    • Add the atchar and any optional ingredients like lettuce, tomato, or a beef patty.

    • Top with the fried egg

  4. Serve:

    • Serve

    • Serve immediately and enjoy your homemade kota.
